# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be respectful. Be constructive. Assume good intent.

## The longer version

This project is maintained by volunteers who care about building useful software and helping others do the same. We expect everyone who participates — in issues, discussions, pull requests, chat, or events — to treat others with respect.

**Expected behaviour:**

- Be kind and patient with people who are learning
- Give constructive feedback — critique ideas, not people
- Accept that others may have different perspectives and experience levels
- Credit people's contributions

**Not acceptable:**

- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory language
- Harassment of any kind, public or private
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions

## Enforcement

If someone's behaviour is making the project unwelcoming, contact [Jan Molak](https://www.linkedin.com/in/janmolak/) directly. Reports will be handled discreetly.

Participants who violate this code may be asked to stop, have their comments removed, or be blocked from the project at the maintainers' discretion.

## Scope

This applies to all Serenity/JS spaces: GitHub repositories, discussions, community chat, social media interactions, and events.
